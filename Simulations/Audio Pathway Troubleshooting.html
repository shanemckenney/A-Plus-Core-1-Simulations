Audio Troubleshooting Simulation

Diagnose and correct endpoint audio issues by validating output, input, permissions, and services in the proper order.

Mode: Practice

How to Use This Simulation

Objective

A user can't be heard, or can't hear others, in a meeting app — walk the audio chain (output, input, permissions, services, app settings) to find and fix the break.

How to Use This Simulator

  1. Work through the controls in order: default playback device, default recording device, mic mute/level, OS microphone privacy permission, Windows Audio services, and the meeting app's own output override.
  2. Use Run Audio Validation as often as you like — it's a self-check that reports problems but does not grade you.
  3. Once validation looks clean, click Submit — this is the only action that's actually graded.
  4. Recommended check order: Output → Input → Permissions → Services → App override.

On Exam Day

Real PBQs test this same layered troubleshooting order, but there's no "Run Validation" safety net — walk the full chain methodically before committing to your final answer.

Scenario

Users in a shared training environment report: “We can’t hear audio in meetings” and “Microphones don’t work during calls.” Your job is to correct the endpoint configuration so playback and recording work reliably.
